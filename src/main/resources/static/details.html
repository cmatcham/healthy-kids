Tips for sleeping well

  • Walk, run, and play with your child.
  • Have fun and be active together as a family.
  • Try a new activity together. Look for low- or no-cost activity ideas on the My Kiwi website.
  • What was fun when you were a kid? Teach these games to your children.
  • Encourage your child to play outside as much as possible.
  • It’s about getting out and about in your local community. Check out your local playgrounds, swimming pools, beach, trees for climbing, walking tracks and parks.
  • You can search for activity ideas in your region at myfamily.kiwi/activities
  • Join a team, cultural groups, or church group (dancing, kapa kaka)
  • Children can have lots of fun with basic equipment such as balls, balloons, kites, buckets, spades, skipping ropes, etc.
  • Instead of short car trips, try walking, biking or scooting with your child.
  • Use your imagination! Build a hut, make an obstacle course, or paint a mural on your fence.
  • Some great indoor activity ideas are dancing, table tennis, skittles, hide and seek, party games (musical statues, musical chairs).
  • Children are smaller than adults so don’t need to eat as much.
  • Have a smaller plate or bowl for your child.
  • Let your child serve themselves.
  • Children’s tastes change, so keep offering food they’ve turned down.s
  • Children don’t need sugary drinks. Water and low-fat plain milk are the best drinks.
  • Eat meals together as a family.
  • Make sure TV and other screens are turned off.
  • Breakfast is a great way to start the day.
  • Choose healthy snacks like fruit, vegetables, low-fat yoghurt, crackers and cheese.
  • Canned and frozen fruit and vegetables are just as good as fresh.
  • Buy fruit and vegetables when they’re in season – they’re cheaper and fresher.
  • Quiet activities such as stories or puzzles are good before bed.
  • Physical activity during the day will help your child sleep at night.
  • As much as possible, have a regular bedtime and wake up time for your child.
  • Your child’s bedtime routine could include cleaning teeth and a wash, changing into nightwear, quiet time (books, song, storytelling, prayer), cuddles and kisses, being tucked in.
  • Children need a regular place to sleep.
  • Their sleeping area should be a place that is dark, warm, dry, cosy, quiet and somewhere they feel safe.
  • Have no distractions in the place where children sleep, including TVs and other screens.
